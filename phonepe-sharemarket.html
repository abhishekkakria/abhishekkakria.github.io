01 • The challenge

India's biggest fintech has its smallest broker

PhonePe launched Share.Market in 2023 - a structural giant that entered the market as a minnow. What PhonePe brings: 500M registered users, 240M monthly actives, over 40% of all UPI volume, an 11-language interface reaching deep into Tier 2/3 India, and a Walmart-backed, SEBI-regulated broking subsidiary under an IPO-bound parent.

Where Share.Market stands: ranked #21 among NSE brokers with roughly 2 lakh active clients, while category leader Groww holds 1.21 crore actives - about 60× larger. And the PhonePe brand means “payments”, not yet “investing”.

The strategic question: how does Share.Market convert pre-existing trust from 500M payments users into investing adoption - with a positioning that is credible, ownable, and sustainable against entrenched leaders?

My role: end-to-end strategy - market analysis, competitive benchmarking, segmentation, positioning framework, and a Year-1 GTM plan with KPIs.

02 • Market context

A once-in-a-generation retail investing boom

Secondary research across SEBI, Mordor Intelligence, IMARC and company filings (FY20–FY25). Demat accounts grew from 41M in FY20 to 192M in FY25 - roughly 30% CAGR, with 41.1M new accounts in FY25 alone, a single-year record.

Growth has left the metros

~45% of new demat accounts now come from Tier 2/3 cities - vernacular-first, first-generation investors.

Literacy gap = product gap

Only 43% of active investors are financially literate; 68% of non-investors cite fear of loss. Guidance beats fees.

Regulation reshaped revenue

SEBI's 2024 F&O reforms cut derivatives volumes ~27%; incumbent broker profits fell 37–60%.

Retention is the new battleground

Active clients fluctuate with markets; accounts opened ≠ accounts used. Habit loops now win.

04 • The insight

The white space: intelligent simplicity

Mapping the category on two axes - mass-market accessibility vs. depth of guidance and intelligence - leaves the top-right quadrant empty: guided, intelligent investing for the mass market. Zerodha sits expert-and-deep; Groww sits mass-market with no guidance; Upstox and Paytm Money sit execution-only. Share.Market can own the open quadrant because of three structural advantages:

Trust, pre-built

Daily UPI habit with 500M users - no rival has a parent-brand trust signal.

Intelligence at zero fee

WealthBaskets (quant-curated portfolios) + Sheets (live-data spreadsheets) - unique in discount broking.

Vernacular reach

11 languages and entrenched Tier 2/3 presence competitors must build from scratch.

06 • Positioning strategy

Three pillars, one ownable idea

Pillar 1

Trust already earned

Inherit PhonePe's daily-habit trust instead of buying awareness. The trust transfer happens through product design, not ad spend - no pure-play broker can replicate it.

Pillar 2

Intelligence, not just execution

Lead with WealthBaskets and Sheets: quant-curated, SEBI-registered guidance at zero brokerage. The place to make smarter decisions - not just place trades.

Pillar 3

Simplicity at every step

Investing should feel like a UPI transfer: 3-minute KYC reusing PhonePe data, one-tap WealthBasket SIPs, portfolio visible beside daily payments.

For everyday Indians who already trust PhonePe with their money but haven't yet invested, Share.Market is the intelligent, guided broking platform that makes equity investing feel as natural as a UPI transfer - combining pre-built trust and 500M-user reach with quant-curated WealthBaskets at zero brokerage, removing both the emotional and financial barriers that keep first-timers on the sidelines.
07 • Go-to-market plan

Product-led acquisition, trust-led conversion

Six priority moves from a 14-recommendation plan - sequenced to exploit the PhonePe distribution advantage first.

Acquire • In-app PhonePe nudges

Contextual prompts at 4 high-intent moments (post-UPI transfer, salary credit, idle balance). Projected CAC ₹500 vs. industry ₹1,200–1,800.

Acquire • Vernacular-first campaign

“Paise bhejo, paise ugao” - original Hindi/Tamil/Telugu content with 10 regional creators per language via Shorts, ShareChat, WhatsApp.

Convert • WealthBaskets as hero

First thing every new user sees: a SEBI-registered, quant-curated portfolio - not a stock ticker. One tap to start a SIP.

Convert • WhatsApp-native referrals

62% of investors act on peer advice. Referral rewards paid as WealthBasket credits; shareable cards show real returns, not promo links.

Retain • RIA endorsement layer

Empanel 500 SEBI-registered advisors; “Advisor-Recommended” badges give sceptical passive investors a professional trust signal.

Retain • Education as a moat

Varsity-style vernacular learning library - builds brand authority independent of the parent over 12–18 months.

Budget logic: ~30% of marketing budget goes to in-app product investment (nudges, onboarding UX) rather than media - the moat competitors can't buy. The mass campaign is held for the PhonePe IPO moment.

Activation over acquisition

Mid-2025 corrections showed platforms losing actives despite huge registered bases. WealthBasket adoption tracks whether the guided-investing positioning is actually landing.

CAC as the strategy's proof point

If in-app nudges work, acquisition cost should run ~60% below industry norms - and a payments-grade brand can't ship a 3.8★ investing experience, so rating guards the trust promise.

What this demonstrates

Share.Market doesn't need to outspend Groww. It needs to out-trust them.
